Zambia Birding Tours

​Zambia, located in the heart of southern Africa, boasts an impressive avian diversity with over 780 recorded bird species, making it a premier destination for birding enthusiasts. The country's varied ecosystems—including miombo woodlands, wetlands, and riverine forests—provide habitats for a multitude of bird species, some of which are endemic or near-endemic. Notably, Zambia is home to two endemic birds: the Chaplin's Barbet and the Black-cheeked Lovebird.

Key birding locations such as South Luangwa, Kafue, and Lower Zambezi National Parks offer exceptional opportunities to observe these species in their natural habitats. Additionally, the Bangweulu Wetlands are renowned for hosting the elusive Shoebill stork, attracting birders from around the globe. With its rich birdlife and diverse landscapes, Zambia promises an unparalleled birding experience for both novice and seasoned enthusiasts.​

Birds I can See on a Zambia Birding Tour

​Embarking on a birding tour in Zambia offers the opportunity to observe a remarkable diversity of avian species across the country's varied habitats. Among the highlights are Zambia's two endemic birds: the Chaplin's (Zambian) Barbet and the Black-cheeked Lovebird. The Chaplin's Barbet, Zambia's only true endemic species, is typically found in the south-central regions, while the Black-cheeked Lovebird inhabits the southwestern parts of the country. ​In addition to these endemics, Zambia is home to several near-endemic and regionally significant species. The elusive African Pitta, renowned for its vibrant plumage and distinctive call, can be spotted in the dense woodlands of the Zambezi Valley. Other notable species include the Böhm's Bee-eater, African Finfoot, Livingstone's Flycatcher, and the African Spotted Creeper. ​Wetland areas, such as the Bangweulu Wetlands, are particularly rich in birdlife, hosting over 400 species. Birders can expect to see a variety of waterfowl, including cormorants, ducks, egrets, geese, herons, ibises, and the sought-after Shoebill. ​Zambia's diverse ecosystems, ranging from miombo woodlands to riverine forests and expansive wetlands, provide habitats for an impressive array of bird species, making it a premier destination for birding enthusiasts.

Zambia Birding Tours Destinations

Bangweulu Wetlands

Located in northeastern Zambia, the Bangweulu Wetlands are renowned for their rich biodiversity and are designated as an Important Bird Area by BirdLife International. This vast ecosystem is home to more than 400 bird species, including significant populations of waterbirds such as herons, egrets, and the vulnerable Shoebill stork. The wetlands also support the endemic Black Lechwe antelope, adding to the area's ecological significance.

Kafue Flats

Encompassing both the Lochinvar and Blue Lagoon National Parks, the Kafue Flats are expansive floodplains that provide critical habitats for numerous bird species. The area is particularly notable for hosting large concentrations of waterfowl and waders, including the Wattled Crane and the Kafue Lechwe, an antelope species endemic to the region. The diverse ecosystems of grasslands and marshes make it a birding hotspot.

Kasanka National Park

Despite its relatively small size, Kasanka National Park boasts an impressive avian diversity with over 330 recorded species. The park's varied habitats, including wetlands, forests, and grasslands, support species such as the Pel's Fishing Owl, African Finfoot, and Ross's Turaco. Notably, Kasanka is famous for hosting the world's largest mammal migration of straw-colored fruit bats, which adds a unique spectacle to the birding experience.

Nkanga River Conservation Area

Situated near Choma in southern Zambia, the Nkanga River Conservation Area is recognized as one of the best sites to observe Zambia's only true endemic bird, the Chaplin's (Zambian) Barbet. The area also supports species like the Crowned Eagle, Miombo Pied Barbet, and Schalow's Turaco, making it a vital destination for birders seeking endemic and region-specific species.

South Luangwa National Park

Known for its rich wildlife, South Luangwa National Park also offers exceptional birding opportunities with over 400 species recorded. The park's riverine forests and lagoons attract species such as the Southern Carmine Bee-eater, African Skimmer, and various raptors. The combination of abundant birdlife and the chance to observe large mammals makes it a popular destination for safari enthusiasts.

Lavushi Manda National Park

Located in the Muchinga Province, Lavushi Manda National Park encompasses miombo woodlands and rocky outcrops, providing habitats for species like the Black Eagle, Augur Buzzard, and various miombo specialists such as the Miombo Rock Thrush and Anchieta's Barbet. The park's diverse terrain offers a rewarding experience for birders interested in woodland species.

Victoria Falls Area

Beyond the breathtaking scenery of Victoria Falls, the surrounding area is rich in birdlife. Species such as the Schalow's Turaco, Rock Pratincole, and African Black Swift can be observed. The nearby Machile River is also a known habitat for the Black-cheeked Lovebird, adding to the region's ornithological appeal.

Mutinondo Wilderness

Nestled on a plateau approximately 30 kilometers west of the Luangwa Valley, Mutinondo Wilderness spans over 10,000 hectares of pristine miombo woodland, interspersed with expansive grasslands and striking granite inselbergs. This privately owned conservation area is celebrated for its remarkable biodiversity and has been designated an Important Bird Area, boasting a recorded 362 bird species as of 2017. Birding enthusiasts can explore a variety of habitats within Mutinondo, each offering unique avian sightings. The miombo woodlands are particularly renowned as one of the best locations globally to observe the elusive Bar-winged Weaver. Other notable species in this habitat include the Racket-tailed Roller, Whyte's Barbet, Miombo Pied Barbet, Anchieta's Barbet, Miombo Tit, Pale-billed Hornbill, Miombo Wren-Warbler, and the African Spotted Creeper. The dambo grasslands and wetland areas within the reserve provide opportunities to spot species such as the Hartlaub's Marsh Widowbird, Stout Cisticola, Fawn-breasted Waxbill, and, with some luck, the elusive Chestnut-headed Flufftail. Additionally, the gallery forests are home to birds like the Schalow's Turaco, Ross's Turaco, Black-backed Barbet, Laura's Woodland Warbler, and Bocage's Akalat. ​Mutinondo Wilderness offers visitors the freedom to explore its diverse landscapes through well-marked trails, making it a haven for birdwatchers seeking both adventure and tranquility in Zambia's natural beauty.

Best time to go on a Zambia Birding Tour

​The optimal time for a birding tour in Zambia is during the wet season, also known as the 'Emerald Season,' which spans from November to April. This period coincides with the arrival of both Palearctic and intra-African migratory birds, enhancing the diversity of species present. Additionally, many resident birds engage in breeding activities during these months, often displaying vibrant plumage and engaging in distinctive mating behaviors, providing exceptional viewing opportunities for birding enthusiasts. However, it's important to note that the increased rainfall can render certain areas less accessible, and some lodges may close during this season. Therefore, planning ahead and consulting with local tour operators can help ensure a rewarding birding experience.
