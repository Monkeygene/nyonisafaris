Birding at Bird Island Nature Reserve

Bird Island Nature Reserve in Lambert’s Bay, South Africa, is a premier birding destination renowned for its accessibility and vibrant seabird colonies. Connected to the mainland by a breakwater, this 3‑hectare island offers an unparalleled opportunity to observe the endangered Cape Gannet up close. As one of only six breeding sites globally for this species, and the only one open to the public, Bird Island holds significant ecological importance.

The reserve also supports populations of Crowned Cormorants and Cape Cormorants, contributing to its status as a key biodiversity area. The island’s rocky terrain and proximity to the nutrient-rich Benguela Current create ideal conditions for seabird nesting and feeding.

Visitors can explore the island via a well-maintained path leading to a bird hide, which allows for discreet observation of the bustling colonies. The best time to visit is during the breeding season, from September to January, when the island is alive with the sights and sounds of nesting birds.

Why Bird Island is a Must-Visit

Bird Island’s unique combination of accessibility, ecological significance, and immersive birding experiences makes it a must-visit for both local and international birders. The breakwater path and elevated hide provide up-close views without disturbing the birds.

Key Species on the Island

  • Cape Gannet: Endangered; one of only six global breeding sites, with public access.
  • Crowned Cormorant: Locally breeding species, often seen perched on rocky outcrops.
  • Cape Cormorant: Abundant in the colony, distinguishable by its glossy black plumage.

Visiting Information

  • Access: Via breakwater from Lambert’s Bay; path is well-maintained and suitable for most visitors.
  • Facilities: Bird hide overlooking the main colony; informational signage along the path.
  • Best Time: Breeding season (September–January) for peak activity.
  • Tips: Bring binoculars, a camera with telephoto capability, and wear comfortable shoes; respect the hide rules to avoid disturbance.

Final Thoughts

Bird Island Nature Reserve offers a rare chance to experience seabird colonies at arm’s length in a safe, sustainable environment. Pair your visit with nearby Lambert’s Bay salt pans or Rocherpan Nature Reserve for a comprehensive West Coast birding adventure.
