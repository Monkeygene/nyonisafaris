Birding at Verlorenvlei Estuarine Lake

Nestled along South Africa’s West Coast between Elands Bay and Redelinghuys, Verlorenvlei is a hidden gem for birders seeking both diversity and serenity. This 13.5 km-long estuarine lake is one of the country’s few coastal freshwater systems, fringed by reedbeds, salt marshes, and fynbos-covered hills. Its tranquil waters mirror the sky, while the surrounding landscape transitions from succulent strandveld to dry mountain fynbos, creating a rich mosaic of habitats.

Recognized as a Ramsar Wetland of International Importance since 1991 and designated as an Important Bird and Biodiversity Area (IBA), Verlorenvlei supports over 189 bird species, including 75 waterbirds. Notably, it occasionally hosts more than 4,000 birds, with the highest recorded count being 11,891. This ecological haven is particularly significant for species like the Great White Pelican, with at least 26% of the Western Cape's population congregating here at times.

Birders can anticipate sightings of Greater and Lesser Flamingos, African Fish Eagles, and a variety of waterfowl. The reserve's diverse habitats and seasonal variations offer unique birding experiences year-round. Whether you're a seasoned ornithologist or a casual birdwatcher, Verlorenvlei promises an immersive encounter with avian life in a setting of remarkable natural beauty.
