Cape to Kruger Birding Tour

Malawi, the "Warm Heart of Africa," is a hidden gem for birdwatchers, offering a rich diversity of habitats and an impressive array of bird species. From the Miombo woodlands to the high-altitude montane forests, Malawi's birding experience is unlike any other in Africa. Our exclusive 15-day Miombo & Montane Birding Tour takes you deep into the country's prime birding hotspots, where you can encounter a spectacular variety of endemic and sought-after species.

Why go birding in Western Cape?

Malawi boasts over 650 recorded bird species, making it a paradise for both seasoned birders and enthusiastic beginners. With its unique blend of montane forests, Miombo woodlands, wetlands, and lakeshores, the country provides incredible opportunities to spot rare and range-restricted species.

Birding Western Cape highlights

Malawi is home to a variety of incredible bird species. Birdwatchers can look out for Miombo specialists like the Stierling’s Woodpecker, Böhm's Flycatcher, Boulder Chat, and the striking Anchieta’s Sunbird. The Montane Forest Endemics, including the elusive Thyolo Alethe, Olive-flanked Ground Robin, and Yellow-throated Apalis, are also a must-see. Highland and wetland enthusiasts will marvel at the stunning Blue Swallow, White-Tailed Crested Flycatcher, and other highland species. Finally, the Rift Valley offers exciting birding opportunities, with the shores of Lake Malawi being a hotspot for kingfishers, cormorants, and other fascinating water-associated species.

Birding Western Cape Destinations

Malawi offers a variety of exceptional birding destinations. Dzalanyama Forest Reserve is a Miombo birding paradise, home to species such as Souza’s Shrike and the Miombo Pied Barbet. The Thyolo and Mount Mulanje regions are known for their montane forest specials, including the Green-headed Oriole and White-winged Apalis. Nyika National Park, Malawi’s high-altitude jewel, provides sightings of the Montane Widowbird and Scarlet-tufted Sunbird. Meanwhile, Liwonde National Park offers a fantastic mix of riverine, savanna, and wetland birds, including the elusive Pel’s Fishing Owl and African Skimmer.
