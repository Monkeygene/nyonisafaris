Birding in Malawi

by Eugene Booysen

Malawi is one of Africa’s most rewarding yet underrated birding destinations. With over 650 species, including rare and endemic birds, the country offers an unparalleled experience for birdwatchers. Whether you’re exploring the lush miombo woodlands, vast wetlands, or scenic highlands, this guide will help you discover Malawi’s top birding locations, the best times to visit, and essential travel tips for an unforgettable birdwatching adventure.

Rolling grassland on the majestic Nyika Plateau

Birding in Malawi Species Diversity

More than 650 species!

Malawi may not be the first destination that comes to mind for African birding, but with over 650 bird species, it offers incredible opportunities for birdwatchers. From wetlands and miombo woodlands to montane forests and grasslands, the country’s diverse ecosystems support a wide range of birdlife, including both resident and migratory species. Whether you’re an experienced birder looking for rare species or a casual observer enjoying the colorful sights and sounds, Malawi has something to offer.

10% of Malawi’s Bird Species Are Not Found Elsewhere in Southern Africa

Despite having only one true endemic species, the Yellow-throated Apalis, Malawi is home to many bird species that are either rare or difficult to find in other parts of Southern Africa. Birds such as Thyolo Alethe, Green-headed Oriole, White-winged Apalis, and Stierling’s Woodpecker are highly localized, making Malawi a must-visit destination for those looking to expand their life lists with unique species.

Birding in Malawi Locations

Nyika National Park – Highlands and Montane Birding

Located in the north, Nyika National Park is Malawi’s premier birding destination. Its rolling grasslands, montane forests, and miombo woodlands support an impressive diversity of bird species, including Bar-tailed Trogon, Blue Swallow, Sharpe’s Akalat, and Babbling Starling. The park’s elevation and open landscapes also make birdwatching particularly enjoyable, with easy visibility of many species.

Liwonde National Park – Wetlands and Riverine Birds

Situated along the Shire River, Liwonde National Park is one of Malawi’s best locations for both birdwatching and wildlife viewing. The park is home to Pel’s Fishing Owl, Lilian’s Lovebird, Böhm’s Bee-eater, and Brown-breasted Barbet, among many other wetland and savanna species. Birders can explore the park via boat safaris, offering close encounters with birds and large mammals alike.

Zomba Plateau – Forest and Highland Species

The Zomba Plateau is one of the best places to find Malawi’s montane forest specialists. The remaining patches of high-altitude forest support highly sought-after species such as Thyolo Alethe, Malawi Batis, White-winged Apalis, and Green-headed Oriole. The combination of scenic hiking trails and birdwatching opportunities makes this plateau a favorite among birders.

Lake Malawi and Surrounding Areas – Waterbirds and Shorebirds

The massive Lake Malawi, one of Africa’s Great Rift Valley lakes, offers excellent birding along its shores and islands. Waterbirds such as African Fish Eagle, White-backed Night Heron, Lesser Jacana, and various kingfishers thrive here. The lake’s sandy beaches and rocky outcrops also provide ideal habitats for shorebirds and waders, particularly during migration seasons.

Miombo Woodlands – Malawi’s Unique Bird Species

Malawi’s extensive miombo woodlands, particularly in areas like Dzalanyama Forest Reserve, are home to some of the most sought-after species in the region. Birds such as Stierling’s Woodpecker, Souza’s Shrike, Boulder Chat, Olive-headed Weaver, Shelley’s Sunbird, and Lesser Seedcracker make these woodlands an essential stop on any Malawi birding itinerary.

The Best Time to Go Birding in Malawi

Year-Round Birdwatching in Malawi

Malawi offers excellent birdwatching opportunities throughout the year, thanks to its variety of resident species. The country’s stable climate and diverse habitats ensure that birders can enjoy rewarding sightings no matter the season.

November–April: The Peak Season for Migrants and Breeding Plumage

The rainy season (November–April) is the best time for birding in Malawi. During this period, migratory birds arrive, and many resident species display their breeding plumage, making identification easier. Wetland areas and forests are particularly active, with vibrant displays and increased bird activity. However, some areas may be harder to access due to heavy rains.

Why Birding in Malawi Deserves More Recognition

Lesser Crowds Compared to Other African Birding Hotspots

Unlike popular birding destinations like Kenya, Tanzania, or South Africa, Malawi remains relatively undiscovered by large numbers of birdwatchers. This means birders can explore pristine habitats with fewer disturbances and enjoy a more intimate and exclusive birding experience.

Easy Access to Diverse Birding Habitats Within a Small Country

Malawi’s compact size makes it easy to travel between different birding habitats in a short time. Unlike larger countries where long drives are required, in Malawi, birders can experience montane forests, miombo woodlands, wetlands, and savannas all within a single trip.

The Opportunity to Combine Birding with Cultural and Safari Experiences

Beyond birdwatching, Malawi offers a unique chance to experience local culture, visit historical sites, and enjoy classic African safaris. Whether it’s exploring vibrant markets, interacting with friendly locals, or viewing the Big Five in one of the country’s national parks, there’s much more to enjoy beyond birding.

Planning Your Birding In Malawi Trip

Malawi Birding Travel Tips and Entry Requirements

Traveling to Malawi is relatively straightforward. Most visitors will require a visa, which can be obtained on arrival or online. Vaccinations for yellow fever (if coming from an endemic country) and standard travel health precautions are recommended.

Accommodation Options for Birders

Malawi offers a range of accommodation options suited for birders, from luxury lodges in national parks to budget-friendly guesthouses near key birding sites. Lodges in parks like Liwonde and Nyika provide guided birding experiences, while independent birders may prefer self-catering cottages near reserves.

Guided Birding Tours in Malawi vs. Self-Guided Birdwatching

While self-guided birding is possible, hiring a knowledgeable bird guide can greatly enhance your experience. Expert guides can help spot rare species, navigate challenging habitats, and provide insights into bird behavior. Nyoni Birding Tours offer tailored birding safaris across Malawi, ensuring an optimized itinerary for seeing the country’s best birds.

Birding in Malawi Conclusion

Malawi may be overlooked as a birding destination, but its rich diversity, stunning landscapes, and welcoming atmosphere make it a hidden gem for birdwatchers. Whether you’re after rare and localized species, stunning scenery, or a mix of birding and safari experiences, Malawi delivers. With its diverse ecosystems, easy accessibility, and year-round birding potential, now is the time to put Malawi on your birding bucket list!
