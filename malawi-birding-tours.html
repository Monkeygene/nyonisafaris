Malawi Birding Tours

Malawi, known as the "Warm Heart of Africa," is a top birding destination for nature lovers. Despite its small size, Malawi boasts diverse birdwatching habitats, from the Miombo woodlands to the stunning Nyika and Zomba Plateaus. With over 650 bird species, including Malawi’s endemic Yellow-throated Apalis, it's a birding paradise. Explore iconic spots like Dzalanyama Forest and Vwaza Marshes, and other National Parks where birders can also encounter big game. Whether you're a seasoned birder or new to birdwatching, Malawi offers an unforgettable birding safari experience.

Start your journey through Malawi's stunning landscapes! We can also arrange trips to South Luangwa National Park in Zambia or the Maleri Islands on Lake Malawi, where you might spot the elusive African Pitta.

Birds I can See on a Malawi Birding Tour

Going on a birding tour in Malawi offers the opportunity to observe a remarkable diversity of bird species across the country's varied habitats. With over 650 species recorded, including numerous rare and localized birds, Malawi is a true birdwatcher's paradise. In the montane forests of the Zomba Plateau, you may encounter the endemic Yellow-throated Apalis, as well as the elusive Thyolo Alethe and the striking White-winged Apalis. The miombo woodlands of Dzalanyama Forest Reserve are home to specialties like Stierling's Woodpecker, Souza's Shrike, and the enigmatic Boulder Chat. In Liwonde National Park, along the Shire River, sought-after species such as Pel’s Fishing Owl, Lilian’s Lovebird, and Böhm’s Bee-eater can be found amidst rich wildlife. Venturing to the expansive grasslands and montane forests of Nyika Plateau National Park, you might spot the Bar-tailed Trogon, Blue Swallow, and the rare Sharpe’s Akalat. Additionally, Lake Malawi's shores provide habitat for a variety of waterbirds, including the majestic African Fish Eagle. Each of these regions offers unique birding experiences, making Malawi a compelling destination for ornithologists and nature enthusiasts alike.

Malawi Birding Tours Destinations

Dzalanyama Forest Reserve

Located southwest of Lilongwe, Dzalanyama Forest Reserve is renowned for its expansive miombo woodlands, providing some of the best birding experiences in Malawi. Birders can anticipate sightings of miombo specialists such as the Stierling's Woodpecker, Souza's Shrike, and the Miombo Rock Thrush. Mixed-species flocks are common here, often featuring species like the Green-backed Woodpecker, White-crested Helmetshrike, and the Rufous-bellied Tit. The forest's diverse habitat also supports the elusive Lesser Seedcracker and the vibrant Anchieta's Sunbird, making it a must-visit for those seeking miombo endemics.

Liwonde National Park

Situated along the Shire River, Liwonde National Park boasts a striking diversity of habitats, including riverine forests, floodplains, and mopane woodlands. This variety supports over 350 bird species, making it one of Malawi's premier birding locations. Notable sightings include the Böhm's Bee-eater, African Skimmer, Palm-nut Vulture, and the elusive Pel's Fishing Owl. The park is also home to significant populations of raptors, with 26 species recorded, including the Martial Eagle and Dickinson's Kestrel.

Zomba Plateau

Rising to an altitude of 2,085 meters, Zomba Plateau is characterized by montane forests, rolling grasslands, and cascading streams. This diverse environment is home to several rare and endangered bird species, including the White-winged Apalis and the Thyolo Alethe. Birders can also encounter the Schalow's Turaco, Olive-headed Greenbul, and the elusive Orange Ground Thrush. The plateau's unique habitat makes it an essential destination for those seeking highland forest specialties.

Nyika National Park

As Malawi's largest national park, Nyika encompasses over 3,000 square kilometers of rolling grasslands, montane forests, and miombo woodlands. With more than 400 bird species recorded, it serves as a refuge for range-restricted species such as the Black-lored Cisticola, Churring Cisticola, and Chapin’s Apalis. Other notable birds include the Bar-tailed Trogon, Wattled Crane, and the Denham’s Bustard. The park's varied habitats and elevation gradients offer birders a chance to observe a wide array of species in a single location.

Best time to go on a Malawi Birding Tour

​Malawi offers exceptional birdwatching opportunities throughout the year, with approximately 80% of its 650 bird species being residents. However, the optimal period for a birding tour is from late October to April, aligning with the arrival of migratory species from Europe and North Africa, thereby enhancing the diversity of sightings. During these months, many birds are in their breeding plumage, displaying vibrant colors that are particularly appealing to birdwatchers. It's important to note that while this timeframe offers increased bird activity, it also coincides with Malawi's rainy season, which can make certain areas less accessible due to heavy rains and flooding. For those interested in combining birding with game viewing, the dry season from May to October provides better wildlife visibility, though bird diversity may be slightly reduced. Additionally, some lodges, particularly around Lake Malawi, may close from early January to late March due to high rainfall and humid conditions. Therefore, planning your visit between October to December or April to May can offer a balanced experience of favorable weather and rich birdlife.
