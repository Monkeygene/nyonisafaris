Nyika Plateau National Park

Nyika Plateau National Park is one of Malawi’s most breathtaking wilderness areas, offering incredible landscapes, diverse wildlife, and world-class birding opportunities. Covering 3,200 km², this high-altitude paradise is Malawi’s largest national park, stretching across the Nyika Plateau and even extending into Zambia. Known for its rolling grasslands, misty hills, and seasonal wildflowers, Nyika is a hidden gem for nature lovers, safari enthusiasts, and birdwatchers alike.

Walking to a Montane Forest patch in Nyika Plateau National Park

Where is Nyika National Park?

Location

Nyika National Park is located in northern Malawi, approximately 480 km north of Lilongwe and 60 km from Rumphi. It sits on the Nyika Plateau, a granitic dome of rolling hills and deep valleys, forming a unique highland ecosystem. The park shares a border with Zambia, with a small 80 km² portion also designated as Nyika National Park in Zambia. Access to Nyika is mainly via a single dirt road that starts in Rumphi, winding through steep escarpments before reaching Chelinda, the park’s headquarters.

Habitat

The Nyika Plateau National Park in Malawi is unlike any other African safari destination. Sitting at an altitude of 2,500m (8,000ft), the park is often shrouded in mist and experiences cooler temperatures than the lowlands below. The unique environment supports a mix of montane grasslands, evergreen forests, and seasonal wetlands, creating the perfect habitat for diverse wildlife and plant life. One of the most fascinating features of Nyika is its over 200 species of orchids, making it the richest orchid hotspot in south-central Africa. The grasslands burst into color during the rainy season (January to April), while Brachystegia woodlands and Protea scrublands dominate the lower escarpments.

Nyika National Park Animals

Nyika National Park offers incredible wildlife viewing, with large herds of antelope, including eland, the largest antelope species in Africa, and roan antelope, which are commonly found in sizeable groups. Zebra are often seen near Chelinda Lodge, while bushbuck, reedbuck, duiker, and warthogs are also frequently spotted across the park. Predators in Nyika include leopards, with the park having one of the highest leopard densities in central Africa, although sightings remain elusive due to their nocturnal nature. Hyenas and jackals are occasionally seen, along with smaller wild cats, civets, and genets. Larger mammals such as elephants are frequently encountered, though buffalo are rare. Birding in Nyika Plateau National Park

Birding Diversity in Northern Malawi

Nyika National Park is a birdwatcher’s paradise, boasting over 400 recorded species, making it one of the top birding destinations in Malawi. Among the most sought-after birds is the rare and striking Denham’s bustard, as well as the wattled crane, a majestic wetland species. The red-winged francolin, endemic to Nyika, is a must-see for birding enthusiasts. The park’s forested areas are also home to elusive species like the moustached green tinkerbird, bar-tailed trogon, starred robin, and Cape batis. While birding in Nyika is rewarding throughout the year, the best time to see migratory birds is between October and January, when the park’s avian diversity reaches its peak.

Best Time to Visit Nyika National Park

Best Time to Visit Nyika National Park

  • December – March (rainy season): Best for wildflowers, orchids, and lush green landscapes.
  • April – October (dry season): Ideal for wildlife viewing and 4x4 safaris.
  • October – January: Peak season for migratory birds.

How to Get There

Nyika can only be accessed from the Malawian side. Visitors can reach the park via:

  • Lilongwe – Mzuzu – Rumphi – Nyika Plateau (best for 2WD in the dry season)
  • Chipata (Zambia) – Lundazi – Nyika Plateau (requires 4WD, especially in the wet season)
  • Mpika (Zambia) – Isoka – Muyombe – Nyika Plateau
  • A 4WD is highly recommended during the rainy season (December–March) as roads can become impassable.

Why Nyika National Park is an Important Birding Destination

Nyika’s open grasslands and forested valleys make it one of the easiest places for birding in Malawi. Unlike dense woodlands where birds can be hard to spot, Nyika’s open terrain allows for clear views of birds in their natural habitat.

Easy Birding in a Spectacular Setting

Nyika National Park is a hidden gem in Malawi’s safari scene, offering incredible scenery, unique wildlife, and world-class birdwatching. Whether you’re interested in trekking, 4×4 safaris, or simply soaking in the misty landscapes, Nyika is a must-visit destination. The park’s rich biodiversity, rare orchids, and abundant birdlife make it an unforgettable safari experience in the Warm Heart of Africa.
